Sunless (or Sunny), is the main character of Shadow Slave. Growing up in the outskirts and leading a life of deceit and focusing on his own survival, he initially has a bleak outlook on life, even resigning himself to his fate when he was infected by the Nightmare Spell. But then, inside his First Nightmare, he resolved to himself that he must survive, no matter what.[1][57]

Debut - Waking World[]

He is a frail-looking young man with pale skin and dark circles under his eyes. He was unhealthily thin and pale, looking haggard in cheap clothes due to lack of sleep.[1] After surviving his First Nightmare, the changes in his physique were subtle but noticeable. His pale skin seemed a little healthier, his muscles a bit more pronounced. He looked slender and lean instead of emaciated and frail, as he did before. There was a slight luster to his dark hair and a shine to his eye.[48]

Debut - Dream Realm[]

He had changed a lot since the events in the Labyrinth. His face was much sharper and angular now, the last remnants of youthful roundness chased away from it by the harsh gauntlet of the Labyrinth. It was thin and pale, with dark circles under his eyes and other signs of extreme exhaustion. His black hair was longer, falling over his eyes in a dirty mess. What changed the most were the eyes themselves. Deep in their dark depths, there was a glint of calm, heavy, bleak coldness that had not been there before. He, too, now possessed the look of a seasoned fighter. The type of calculating coldness hiding in the eyes of such men was known as the "murder math" in the outskirts. He had the eyes of a killer and even deeper than that, visible only to him, golden threads of Weaver's inhuman legacy glowed mysteriously in the darkness.[58]

He is a cynical, disillusioned young man with a bleak outlook on life. Despite this, he harbors a sense of humor and a strong desire to not only survive but also thrive in this world. During his First Nightmare, he exhibited caution and devious cunning that allowed him to survive it,[57] earning him praise from the Spell, "Your treachery truly knows no bounds".[55] He is smart and capable of being cold and ruthless when the situation asks for it.[76][17] In the academy, he discovered a couple of new things about his self, deep down he was apparently rude arrogant and more than a little bit lustful.[77] He is so adept at deception that he earned the reputation of being a pathological liar, and additionally, he is a paranoid person.[77][78][79]

Initially, he has always strived to be self-sufficient, not allowing himself to depend on anyone. In his mind, this was the true meaning of strength. However, he changed his mind after fighting together with Nephis in the Dream Realm.[80] He had changed without even noticing. Somehow, he had grown unsatisfied with his previous way of life — one where his sole goal of survival at all costs overshadowed everything else. Whether he lived or died had always been the only thing that mattered. But now, "how" he lived mattered more.[81] He is a very rapacious person, but he knows how to keep his avarice in check most of the time.[82][83][84] After leaving the ancient stronghold, his behavior became highly erratic and a bit crazy.[85]

His mental state had suffered greatly because of a number of reasons:[86]

  • The fact that there was no Gateway in the Bright Castle. It turned out that everything he had endured to make it to the Dark City, the crushing intensity of their journey through the Labyrinth, the diabolical trap of the Soul Devourer and their escape from it, and the cold and harrowing night of traversing the dark sea, were all for nothing.
  • The feeling of alienation he felt while living with Nephis, Cassie, and all the newcomers in the outer settlement.[87]
  • The terrible epiphany of what Cassie's vision really meant.[88]
  • The fatal mistake in the conversation with Harper and the brutal murder that followed. [89]
  • Nephis, after her powerful declaration to destroy the Spell, made it impossible for him to trust her any longer.[90]
  • The excruciating, never-ending sea of torturous pain he suffered after being mortally wounded by the Black Knight.[91]

Sunny used the shield of madness to save himself from the bottomless despair that threatened to destroy him completely. It protected him from having to look at the heartbreaking truth and remember it. Additionally, he has the habit of making himself an outcast to prevent anyone from making him one.[86]

Volume 3[]

Even though he wasn't the same penniless kid anymore, he was an Awakened, one marked as a special strategic asset by the government and was considerably wealthy, connected, and even powerful. Deep down, he still believed that the statement held true. His clothes and circumstances might have changed, but Sunny himself remained the same. Twisted and damaged, as anyone who had seen the truth of this world would be.[92]

Now that he had risen above his previous pathetic self, he had a whole swarm of new, much more serious problems. Nephis, the Sovereigns, the legacy of the mysterious Weaver, and all of these things promised a future full of turmoil and danger. He was not exactly the kind of person who had good things to offer to anybody.[92]

Despite how complicated his relationship with Cassie is, he could put away his resentment for the sake of mutual benefit. He's a very pragmatic person when he needs to be.[93] He was really bad at dealing with his feelings and for him, fighting hordes of Nightmare Creatures was much easier.[94]

In the waking world, Sunny exhibited being a petty person. An example of this was when he ate a juicy peach with gusto in front of Cassie without offering one to the latter.[95] Another was when Rain laughed at him when he said he could make Kai teach her how to shoot a bow personally, causing Rain to taunt him to might as well invite him to dinner which he actually did.[96][97]

Volume 4[]

His mind possessed a remarkable flexibility to it, an insidious ability to adapt to any circumstance almost in an instant, as though it was formless and shapeless by nature, and thus easily molded to fit any situation.[98]

He had grown up in the outskirts, fighting for his life in a cruel, indifferent world. He had to learn a lot of vicious lessons to survive. Those lessons made him selfish, cynical, and jaded, unwilling to trust anyone or believe in anything.

Those qualities had helped him to remain alive, but as his life changed, some of them turned into a hindrance. Slowly and painfully, he had shed his previous skin and learned new things. He learned to trust those who deserved to be trusted, and have hope in himself and the future.

However, one thing he never managed to leave behind — and did not really want to — was his spite. Spite had been the only thing that motivated him to survive the First Nightmare, after all. Back then, and maybe even now, it was his only reason to live.

— Chapter 728 Spiteful Shadow
He believed in the value of retribution, "An eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth", which was the ancient law. He was also a very stubborn person. He wanted fate to go to hell and prove that once and for all, he was not a marionette that hung powerlessly from the strings of fate only able to dance to a predetermined tune.[99]

According to Effie, as long as he lacks conviction, he can never be stronger than Nephis.[100] He might not have been the most principled person, but only a few could boast of having more determination than him. Otherwise, he would not have survived all the terrible trials that life had thrown his way.[101]

His own goals are to have a good life, to be free, and to make sure that those he cared about were safe. Although his desires were centered around himself, he considers them a great virtue.[101]

Usually, Sunny felt a lot of negative emotions every time he thought about the Innate Ability of his Aspect, Shadow Bond, which tied him to Changing Star's will. However, after he learned how he and Nephis can augment each other, his curiosity and greed for power briefly overpowered his resentment.[102]

He can maintain composure despite seething emotions, honed through years of battles and conflicts.[103]

He cared deeply for his sister, willingly providing her with a handicap, believing she didn't have to grow strong or die, and seeing himself as capable of being strong for both of them.[104]

He admitted that he was contrarian and antisocial enough to balance the agreeableness of people who chose the life of service.[105] Apparently, both Nephis and Obel thinks that he underestimates himself and his abilities.[106][107]

He felt indignation as he witnessed the deaths of good men and women in Antarctica, while powerful ones were absent. Despite leading the caravan and pretending to be an intrepid commander, he felt much more comfortable living alone in the dark.[108]

Previously, Sunny held contempt for Legacies, believing they were arrogant and hateful, someone who did not deserve the unfair privileges bestowed upon them by nothing except for birthright. However, he eventually valued and admired some of them.[109]

In the end, he did not find his conviction and did not need it. All he ever wanted was to live a good life and keep the people he cared about safe. He hated the Sovereigns because of the world they built, and their pursuit of conviction which trampled millions in the process.[110]

Volume 6[]

Although Sunny was aware of his strength, he also understood its limitations. The Winter Beast had given him a painful lesson on the subject.[111]

Sunny contemplated his life without lying, imagining if it would have been easier or harder. He would not have become enslaved but would have never gotten close to others, choosing to lie and manipulate them instead of trusting them. This fear of rejection and betrayal would probably lead to his death or at least turn into something ugly and twisted.[112]

Sunny, who once wanted to be as strong as Changing Star, now realizes his understanding of real strength has been misguided.[113]

In the past, Sunny had tried to remain under the radar by hiding his power to appear weak and insignificant. After arriving in Antarctica, he was forced to reveal enough of his strength to avoid being perceived as weak. Now, he needs a new disguise. Sunny's goal is to deceive the representatives of the great clans and gain respect for his abilities.[114]

Sunny knew how precious and fragile trust was. He had taken time to extend his own trust toward Effie and Kai, but his friends respected his silence and did not push him to reveal his secrets.[115]

Sunny had been aiming to achieve his freedom which had his real goal all along. However, he found himself bound to more people, places, and things than ever before. He was bound by his own desires. This realization led him to question the power of the Demon of Desire, which had been one of the most powerful among the daemons, except for Weaver. He considered whether being bound to people, ideas, and values was a good thing, as he had chosen these bonds himself. Despite this, a small part of Sunny rebelled against this, wanting to be free and unrestrained. He had to finish his mission, which was to evacuate the citizens of Antarctica, and then explore other options. Despite his distant dreams, Sunny remained calm and determined, possibly recognizing his true conviction.[116]

Volume 7[]

Before, Sunny had been a powerless street rat who only cared about himself and how to keep his secret. Now, the scrawny kid from the outskirts was gone, replaced by a fair young man with eyes so cold and dark that it was easy to cut yourself on their chilling gaze.[117]

He put a lot of meaning in personal strength, which is not unwise, but it serves as an artificial limit. He learned from Neph's Second Nightmare that personal strength is important but pales in comparison to true power. In the Third Nightmare, Sunny realized that empowering his allies, such as Nephis, Cassie, Effie, Kai, Jet, and Mordret, were as much a part of his power as his Shadows.[118]

His Flaw had landed him in trouble, but it also benefited him. Without the Flaw, he would have continued to lie and deceive, remaining isolated and untrusting. The mindset that saved his life in the outskirts would have become the end of him in the perilous world of the Spell. He would not have grown close to others and made friends, potentially leading to his death in a ditch, miserable and alone.[119]

Sunny was not the most emotionally intelligent person, or at least he had not been before. If anything, his emotional development had been stunted — first by the rough life in the outskirts, then by the ruthless reality of the Nightmare Spell.

— Chapter 1432 Admission

Sunny had complicated relationships with his closest companions, Nephis, and Cassie. They have been in a tangled mess of emotions and unspoken things, resulting in a strained and emotionally charged relationship for a long time, especially with Nephis. It took him a long time to recognize how he felt about Nephis.[33]

In Chapter 1478, Sunny recognizes that his desire to protect his companions is more powerful than his vindictive desire to avenge himself.

Sunny had long realized that his allies were a part of his power but still felt disappointed in himself for not doing well in the Nightmare, as he had performed exceptionally well until the battle in Twilight happened. Though experiencing a setback was a change of pace.[120]

Volume 8[]

Sunny felt more at home in the Dream Realm as a Transcendent. Despite the world's rejection of him, remained determined to live, even more than ever before. His personality quirk was that he wanted to survive out of spite no matter the cost.[121]

Sunny believes that knowledge is the origin of power, but it is also the heaviest thing in the world.[122]

He is trying to find lost knowledge and forbidden things, such as the Doom War, the Nightmare Spell, and the purpose of the Nightmare Spell. He also wants to find the remaining parts of Weaver's forbidden lineage, as he is not satisfied with having an incomplete collection of fragments.[122]

In Chapter 1690, there was a shift in Sunny's perception of power after becoming Transcendent, which led him to understand that truly powerful beings don't rely on external weapons or tools for strength. Instead, they recognize that they are the source of power, and those tools merely serve to channel their inherent strength, rather than provide external might.

He was a self-centered individual who prioritized himself over others. There were a few people out there he would die for, but it was because they were dear to him, and their loss would hurt him terribly. Selflessness is only attainable for those who have not suffered enough pain and anguish. People who had experienced anguish and pain knew the value of selfishness because caring about oneself meant not having to suffer that anguish again.[123]

Sunny endured great tragedies to continue his journey. He couldn't remain unmoved despite his world's destruction and species extinction. Volume 10[]

After becoming a Supreme, Sunny occasionally found himself lost—particularly when his focus slipped. In those moments, the overwhelming influx of sensory input from the countless shadows under his control blurred his sense of identity, making it difficult to remember his humanity. In reality, he was no longer truly human. Although he tried to distinguish himself from the shadows, the truth was that he was no more than a shadow himself—albeit a divine shadow.[74] (Refer to Sunny's Process to Natural Supremacy)

Sunny was experiencing a complex mix of emotions. On the surface, he felt warmth and joy while teasing Kai—a fleeting return to a familiar, comforting dynamic that allowed him to momentarily forget his painful reality of being forgotten. There was a sense of regained companionship that brought him hope. However, this sweetness was tinged with a deep, lingering bitterness. Being around people he once shared strong bonds with—Effie, Jet, Cassie, and even Nephis—highlighted the ache of incompleteness in his current relationships. Despite being present and still able to protect and connect with them, it felt like something vital was missing. Sunny yearned not just to be there but to be truly known, remembered, and loved. His reflections revealed an inner turmoil driven by his desire for recognition and belonging, clashing with the harsh truth that no one could fully know or love him because of his unique, fateless existence and the realization that being loved and worship of humanity would become its doom. While he usually managed to suppress these feelings and enjoy the moments he had, recent revelations about his future forced him to confront the inevitability of isolation tied to his freedom. This led to a somber realization: freedom, for him, was inherently lonely.[126]

Sunny felt a deep sense of disconnection from his humanity, brought on by his prolonged experience of living through multiple bodies and perspectives. While being alone in his own mind again was both comforting and unsettling, it made him realize how fundamentally his consciousness had changed. He recognized that his mind was no longer purely human—not just in form, but in essence—shaped by the unnatural breadth and repetition of his experiences. This transformation wasn't about losing emotion like Nephis, but about evolving into something beyond human due to the sheer scale and variety of his perceptions, raising unsettling questions about identity and what it means to remain human.[127]

He felt a profound awareness of his ongoing transformation—both a promising step toward Apotheosis and a deeply unsettling erosion of his humanity. Though still young, he was already witnessing how much he had changed and feared becoming like the Supreme humans he despised, such as Anvil and Ki Song—powerful but detached and indifferent to human suffering. He missed his True Name, once a grounding force, and feared the loss of self in endless change. Yet amid this unease, Sunny found comfort in the one constant of his life: his unwavering love for Nephis. Despite all the trials and separations, their bond endured, and he realized that while he saw himself as her anchor to humanity, she might also be his.[127]

Sorcery[]

Sunny inherited the intrinsic predisposition to weaving from the Demon of Fate. He was able to intuitively sense the purpose of spellweave elements due to his connection to Weaver. This talent was literally in his blood.[135]

After knowing the name of the memory, the patterns became easier to discern, and weaving through every part of the tapestry of ethereal strings was the same name that serves as the bridge and unifying element between them.[135] Knowing that the name of a Memory was integral to its spellweave, allowed Sunny to see the pattern as a whole and was finally able to change the memories without destroying them entirely.[136] Learning how to weave runes required both a good understanding of weaves and a deep knowledge of the runic language the Spell used.[137]

Sunny's knowledge of weaving had mostly come from studying the weaves created by the Nightmare Spell, and the Memories created by the enchanters of Valor were also enchanted using unique Aspects.[138]

Sunny's skill as a sorcerer had come from comparing the patterns of different enchantments to each other and trying to deduce the common logic behind them.[138] He was more of a copycat than an actual sorcerer. His repertoire consisted of recreating the weaves of various enchantments he had gleaned from his own Memories rather than creating them himself. He had learned the fundamental principles of sorcery through Blood Weave and the ability to see weaves. His achievements were impressive compared to other modern Awakened, but he was still a novice.[139]

By learning an entirely different school of weaving using Ananke's dark mantle, his ability to compare and deduce the fundamental principles of sorcery could take a qualitative leap. Studying the differences and commonalities between the weaves allowed him to learn the unfamiliar ways of its creator and push his own skill forward.[138]

Will[]

Main article: Will

Sunny's Will is described as subtle and insidious, akin to a weapon that only knows how to kill, as Nephis once remarked. However, this metaphor only scratches the surface — his Will is flexible and versatile, willing to assume any form necessary to defeat the enemy. Just like shadows are inherently formless, Sunny's spirit mirrors that nature, making him uniquely capable of channeling foreign concepts and adapting to various forms of power. This adaptability is deeply tied to the art of Shadow Dance, particularly its evolving steps.[143]

He has developed the ability not only to sense the essence and battle style of his enemies, but also to perceive their Will — its impact and affinity. This skill was honed through deep insight and experience, rather than innate talent alone.[143]

Despite his growing power, Sunny struggles with the danger of losing his sense of self, especially in the absence of his True Name. Without it, fully embracing foreign concepts or forms risks his identity dissolving completely, a price that grows heavier the more powerful he becomes. Thus, his Will is both a strength and a liability — a weapon finely tuned to kill, but vulnerable to self-erasure if overextended.[143]

Weaponize Will

Sunny had taken the first steps during his battle with Anvil[144][145][146], drawing inspiration from the King of Swords, and in the time since, he had grown more adept at channeling his Will.[146]

However, a true Supreme Art demanded more — it required his Will to become a direct force, expressed through his body with no dilution. The battle with the Millipede Queen revealed a vital insight:[146]

  • Will lost strength when passed through intermediaries.[146]
  • Slayer's arrow, though crafted by Sunny and powered by both their Wills, dealt only modest damage because it had traveled too far from its source.[146]
  • Will was most potent when transmitted directly — through bare hands, a held sword, or any medium intimately connected to the user.[146]

In Chapter 2362, Sunny used his mastery of Shadow Dance and the transformation of his Will to challenge a being of greater rank. Rather than confronting the Wolf head-on, he invoked the deep memory of a primeval hunter, channeling that ancient essence into himself. Through sheer force of Will, he spiritually transformed, not just mimicking a form but becoming a different being — a vengeful chieftain driven by loss and rage.
