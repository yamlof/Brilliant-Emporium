Debut - Waking World[]

Nephis was a tall, slender girl around Sunny's age, with clear grey eyes and a detached look on her face. She had silver-white hair that was cut short and neatly parted to the side.[18]

Debut - Dream Realm[]

The first time Sunny met Nephis in the Forgotten Shore, she was tall and lithe, with silky silver hair and grey eyes. Her clothes were made of black seaweed, leaving her fair skin and athletic build exposed. She was poised, alert, and barefoot.[3]

When Sunny saw Nephis again after three months of separation, she changed a lot compared to the last time he had seen her. Outwardly, she looked almost the same — tall, firm, and strangely distant, as though existing slightly apart from the rest of the world. She was wearing the Starlight Legion Armor, which accentuated the graceful lines of her slender, lithe body.[19] She has ivory skin.[20]

Her silver hair was much longer, almost reaching her shoulders. Without the short tomboyish cut, she looked strangely mature and feminine, making Sunny's heart beat a little faster. Her calm grey eyes were as striking as ever. It shined with resolve and determination, radiating an almost infectious feeling of temperate confidence.[19]

During the Beach Episode, her figure seemed especially slender and lithe next to Effie. However, she was also extremely athletic in appearance. Her ivory skin was pleasantly contrasted against the olive skin of Effie, making it a sight to behold.[20]

After staying behind the Dream Realm, somewhere far away, a young woman with striking grey eyes, was battered by the heat. Her skin was covered in terrible burns, her silver hair dirty with blood, and surrounded by a radiant halo of reflected light. She wore charred remains of shattered armor and wielded a broken sword, its silver blade fractured and ending in a jagged edge close to the hilt.[21]

A radiant figure slowly rose from the white flames, wreathed in blinding light. The flame ghost — or whatever the being was — seemed beautiful and pure, as if untouched by the imperfections of the world. After two years of wandering the Dream Realm in solitude and relentless battles for survival, Nephis came back to the waking world as an Ascended.[22]

Later, she was seen wearing a simple government-issued tracksuit. Her silver hair, however, was now long and lustrous, framing her face and making her look strangely feminine. Her beauty was stunning. When she appeared, it seemed as though the whole hallway became a little brighter.[23] According to Rain, she was a slender and graceful young woman, with flawless ivory skin, striking grey eyes, and strange silver-white hair color. Her face was calm, aloof, and strangely mesmerizing.[24]

When Effie arranged an outing, she used Autumn Leaf lent by Sunny to change the color of her distinct silver hair. Her hair was now black and lustrous, falling to the middle of her back like a waterfall of the finest silk. Her calm grey eyes contrasted against it which seemed even more striking. Her ivory skin seemed to glow despite not channeling her white flames. She looked stunning wearing a vibrant red dress that barely reached the middle of her thigh and left her shoulders bare. These clothes were picked by Effie.[25]

For the Valor clan ball, Nephis chose to wear a more practical attire, but nevertheless splendid. Her knightly suit consisted of black trousers, a black doublet with silver embroidery, and a pristine white cape. Her hands were clad in white gloves. The simple band of the Crown of Dawn accentuated the steely grey of her striking eyes.[26]

When dressed in house Valor's colors, Nephis appeared differently. The black armor created a striking contrast against her ivory skin, silver hair, and cool grey eyes. She looked like a beautiful dark spirit that took the form of a deadly knight to sow the seeds of discord and ruin in the mortal world.[27]

Transcendent[]

Nephis' presence was stronger and more tantalizing than before, and she had grown even more beautiful after undergoing the rebirth of Transcendence. Her presence, which seemed to illuminate the world, was stronger and more tantalizing than before, with wings extended from her back. Her wings were too majestic and pure to be part of the mortal world.[28] Her presence has a profound impact on people's souls, igniting their innermost hopes and aspirations.[29] (See also: Transformation Ability)

In Chapter 1688, Nephis was wearing a simple, white dress that covered her shoulders and had a modest neckline, the hem resting somewhat high above her knees. The modesty accentuated her slender body and the stark contrast between her alabaster skin and black hair only made her grey eyes look more striking.

During the beach date with Sunny, beneath her dress, she wore a white swimsuit—not too modest and not too revealing, just enough to leave one's imagination ablaze—her alabaster skin, glistening silver hair, striking grey eyes and the smooth lines of her supple body. Her figure was slender and athletic, with a flat stomach and perfectly defined muscles, as expected from a person who spent most of her time either in battle or practicing sword. Yet, it wasn't harsh and cumbersome, her form remained beautiful and soft in all the right ways—captivating without being overwhelming.[30]

Supreme[]

Nephis is described as a striking and graceful figure. She has silver hair that, while she slept, was scattered across the pillow, framing her beautiful face, which appeared blissfully calm and endearingly vulnerable in rest. Her eyes are a vivid grey, and when she opened them, the sunlight reflected in their depths, making her pupils sparkle with light. Upon rising, her movements were elegant and fluid, revealing a lithe, graceful form that Sunny admired openly. Despite her powerful presence, there's a softness to her—evident in the slight blush that colored her cheeks when teased and the subtle smile she tried to suppress.[31]

Childhood[]

As a little child, Nephis believed the gods were hypocrites, which meant her disdain for authority figures goes back a long time.[32] According to her, she was a very reasonable child and often struggled to understand people through reason. However, she learned that people are inherently unreasonable and that helped her gain some understanding. She also admit to being a bit contradictory as a person.[32]

Volume 1 & 2[]

The first time Sunny saw Nephis, she looked as though she was apart from the world. She looked confident and self-sufficient, but also a bit lonely.[18] Later, Sunny realized that even though she seemed to exist a bit apart from the world, she was mysterious, aloof, and rather cool. Her taciturn character and strange speech patterns made people interacting with her feel unnerved and rattled, often revealing more than they had been planning to. The less she talked, the more she seemed to know. That silent, indifferent confidence was arresting, and sometimes even oppressive.

However, that impression turned out to be completely wrong, as Sunny realized, Nephis was simply an incredibly awkward person. She was not in a habit of openly showing her emotions. As a result, she was so bad at emoting them that her face always looked cold and neutral. That's why, most of the time, she simply chose to talk as little as possible or not to talk at all.[33]

She seemed like a noble person, seeing that she saved Cassie without asking for anything in return, just because she wanted to, and provided the dwellers of the slum in the Dark City with free food.[34][2] Although she seemed noble, she was shown to be cynical and pragmatic as well when she told Sunny to take Cassie in the castle because it was the best choice and they need information on what to do next using Sunny's shadow.[35] Another example was letting Caster close to her because she needed him in the future. It doesn't matter that he intends to betray her.[36]

She also has this maniacal obsession and conviction to accomplish her goal of destroying the Spell that Sunny thought she was insane.[37]

Volume 5[]

According to Effie, Nephis has the conviction that Sunny doesn't have.[38] She disregarded herself to an almost demented degree in pursuit of her goals. All her desires were aimed outside, and incredibly ambitious.[39]

Sunny noted that she seemed strangely stable for a person who had gone through something that no one should have ever been forced to endure.[39]

While she was lost and wandering the Dream Realm, slowly forgetting what it felt like to be human, there was one thing she clung to that reminded her that all her prior life had not been just a dream and that was Sunny or to be exact, his runes.[40]

“
...Because I don't need anyone to follow me against their will. I don't need a magical collar to make people serve me. I don't need slaves. That is not enough for me, Sunny. Why should I settle for submission when I can have loyalty? People who will follow me, serve me, and obey me will do so because that is their most ardent desire. They will do so with a smile. If I ever want to make you mine, Sunny, you will become mine — not because you were forced to, but because you would want to. That... is also a fact.

She didn't need an unwilling slave. She despised the idea of forcing anyone to obey her. Instead, she intended for people to follow her of their own free will. She preferred for people to bind themselves by their desire to serve her. She also valued Sunny and even respected him. But despite all that, she still saw him as a follower and a follower could never be an equal.[41]

She can be an incredibly resourceful individual when necessary, similar to Sunny. This was exhibited when she gained fifteen new Memories in the span of two days to respond to Sunny's question via their bond.[42]

From the outside, she looked like she drew power from conviction but the truth was simpler. She had told Sunny why she wanted to destroy the Spell and that was because she hated it.[43]

Volume 6[]

Nephis was determined to destroy the Sovereigns since they were suppressing humanity's conquest of the Spell and, more importantly, they stood in her way. She didn't care what motivated the Supremes. This same for Sunny.[44]

In Chapter 1128, Sunny admitted that "stern and stoic, vaguely sinister, and completely insane" were all words that could be used to describe Nephis.

Volume 7[]

Before, Nephis had been an isolated young woman who didn't know much about the world, except for the fact that she was going to burn it to the ground. She had changed the most, learning too many bitter lessons.[45]

She did care to those close to her like preventing Sunny to die after his battle with Azure Serpent and the Dread Lord though the how is not what Sunny like.[46][47]

Volume 8[]

Note: The information was from the perspective of Nephis after Sunny became fateless.

Nephis may looked like an emotionless, aloof, and deadpan figure but it was not that she did not have feelings. She had just spent her formative years in solitude, unable to express her emotions in the company of others. Although she now knows how to communicate due to being a public figure, her default state remains unchanged, reverting to her childhood.[48]

For as long as Nephis could remember, after her grandmother's passing, she had been on her own carrying a crushing burden without anyone there to lend her a hand.[49]

Despite the support of the Fire Keepers, Effie, and Kai, they were not as powerful as her and could not lessen her burden. The only exception was Cassie. She might have collapsed long ago without her quiet strength and friendship. But Cassie was her subordinate too and therefore her responsibility.[49]

Her burden was something she had chosen to carry herself, an unreasonable ambition but sometimes feels suffocated by the depth and intensity of her own desire, surrounded by enemies and unable to sleep. She also feels weak, even if she tries not to show it. She wonders if there is someone as powerful as the Lord of Shadows, who shares her hatred towards the Sovereigns, who she could rely on to stand by her in the face of danger. She is left with a sense of longing for someone to rely on and finds herself wanting to, but she is not entirely sure if the Lord of Shadows is a human or if he can be trusted.[49]

She believed that she could rely on herself, as everyone else would either betray her, abandon her, or stay by her side and perish. This belief has persisted since childhood and continues to this day.[49]

Nephis believed that her will, strength, and longing were enough, and she did not need anyone else. However, she had never had an ally or partner. Interestingly, the Lord of Shadows gave her a sense of unexplainable familiarity, making trusting him and in him easy. Nephis was wary of this ease, thinking it might be one of his Aspect Abilities.[49]

Many people thought that she was a cold and emotionless person, but she was a fiery individual with intense emotions, but she rarely displayed them.[50]

In Chapter 1676, Nephis is willing to die for a couple of Sleepers, as she believes it is worth it because her principles are more important than material possessions.

She has been powerless for a long time, witnessing the loss of her family's heirlooms, wealth, home, and the people who surrounded her were either killed or chased away one after another, disappearing without a trace. This scar left a lasting impression on her, making her value her beliefs, principles, and will more than material possessions.[51]

She believes that the things she valued the most were inside of her, and therefore, could never be taken away. However, she fears losing these things, as she could betray herself and discard them. The thought of losing herself is appalling and more terrible than death, and so she would rather die.[51]

Nephis had given up on trying to explain herself to others, but she believed the Lord of Shadows might be able to understand her.[51]

Volume 9[]

Nephis had an indifferent attitude towards pain.[52] In Chapter 1897, she did not even react to her gruesome wound, as if the pain did not matter.

She was once a straightforward person but has come to value deceit and misdirection over the years, despite not being naturally good at it. She learned to use lies by quietly observing people around her, possibly due to being blessed with good teachers. She was able to act convincingly in front of Moonveil enough to launch a surprise attack.[53]

She enjoyed holding a sword, but now she no longer loves it. She used to love swordsmanship because it had a connection to her father, who taught her how to hold a sword. However, she now sees it as a tool, possibly the sharpest, and doesn't know if there's anything left that she loves.[54]

In Chapter 2187, it was mentioned that she was a direct opposite of her mother. While Smile of Heaven was lively and infectious, Nephis is awkward, withdrawn, harsh, and difficult to deal with most of the time. However, despite these differences, she still possesses that rare ability to forge bonds between people who might not have become companions otherwise.[55]

“
She is just like her father, in that regard. Ah… but I think that she resembles her mother much more, instead.
”

― Anvil to Sunny about Nephis,
Chapter 2256 Abominable Curse


According to Anvil, Nephis shared the same relentless drive, ambition, or unwillingness to surrender—a trait that defines Nephis's character in Sunny's eyes. Her refusal to accept limits, her striving toward divinity, and her desire to fully overcome the Nightmare Spell mirror what Anvil likely saw in her father. While he didn't elaborate directly, Anvil's emphasis on her greater resemblance to her mother suggests deeper, possibly more emotional or ideological traits—such as compassion, inner strength, or moral conviction.[56]

Volume 10[]

As Humanity's Supreme, Nephis bore the immense weight of three billion souls while serving as a guardian deity for humanity's warriors fighting Nightmare Creatures across two worlds. Despite her powers struggling to reach Earth, she remained steadfast, constantly using her Aspect—a process that brought her continuous, excruciating pain. Unbeknownst to those who worshiped her as a symbol of hope, this suffering was the hidden cost she paid. The one consolation was that she no longer risked losing her humanity, thanks to Sunny's support, her own efforts to stay grounded, and her deep connection to the emotions and desires of countless people. Still, Sunny resented the self-sacrificial nature of her role, regardless of whether others saw her as a messiah.[57]

Battle Ability

Nephis was able to move with the calm precision of a battle machine. Her technique was clean, graceful, and ruthless. No matter what type of attack was thrown at her, she was able to either predict or instantly react to it, then deflect and turn it against the attacker with a minimum amount of effort.[59] She was swift, calm and precise. Every move she made was calculated and perfectly timed. She innately understood the flow and logic of combat, which gave her the ability to roughly predict what actions the mindless beasts would perform in the next seconds.[60]

Despite not possessing the unnatural ability to peer into a being's essence through Shadow Dance, Nephis can study the enemy and discern their battle techniques. Her insight was not supernatural but valuable, as Nephis was a genius of combat, born and bred for battle, and had a chilling affinity to bloodshed and destruction.[61]

Her favored style was always flowing and unpredictable, but in the Third Nightmare, it had assumed a strange, almost mystical quality. Delivering force is the basis of swordsmanship or spearmanship, or any other kind of weapon skill. The amount of force is dependent on mass and acceleration. The body is the source of that force, and the weapon is a force multiplier. It concentrates it into a narrow tip, thus making it easier to deliver lethal damage.[62]

Nephis' swordsmanship, which had always been somewhat ineffable due to her control over the battlefield and enemy, now possesses a bizarre quality. Her steps, cadence, offensive and defensive moves seem familiar but dangerous. Her light steps allow her to position herself optimally for attacks, while her defense is nearly impenetrable, alternating between immovable hardness and inviting softness, which dissipated the force of attacks.[63]

Nephis could control the flow of combat and manipulate the actions of her enemies which had always been the basis of her skill. As her combat mastery grew rapidly, this ability grew even more frightening.[64]

Sorcery[]

Nephis learned Sorcery of Names from Ananke.[65] Her Shaping was direct and crude, lacking stability but at the same time contained a wild and fearsome will.[66] Her mastery of Shaping was rudimentary, with crude ways of channeling Names and primitive phrases.[52]

In Chapter 2296, Nephis' talent was described as indomitable. Once she learned a True Name, that Name could never escape her. She could invoke any Name without hesitation or failure.

Will[]

Main article: Will

Nephis possesses a singularly powerful Will, described as firm and indomitable. Her spirit is compared to a sword that refuses to break, a force that demands everything else break under its blade. This kind of Will is uncompromising and absolute, rooted in pure conviction and a relentless drive to achieve her purpose, no matter the cost.[67]

Unlike Sunny's adaptable nature, Nephis' Will is defined by unshakable clarity and force. She does not seek to bend or transform — instead, she cuts through all obstacles with unwavering determination. Her Will represents purity of intent, with no room for flexibility or doubt.[67]

Survival Skills[]

Nephis, as the heir of the Legacy Clan, had to learn to take care of herself in harsh conditions from childhood. Nutrition is an integral part of survival, so it got a lot of attention in their curriculum. But each clan has its own way of teaching its heirs. She has been taught both theoretical and practical skills concerning proper nutrition through a series of thorough and elevated courses.[68]

Background

Her grandfather, Immortal Flame was the first one to conquer the Second Nightmare and become a Master.[17] And her mother, Smile of Heaven received her True Name in her First Nightmare.[72] She became hollow while pregnant with Nephis. Making her a hollowborn.[73]

She was four when her father died.[74] There is a manor which is the only property her clan has left and everything else was liquidated and sold a long time ago.[75] That is why she doesn't have any Memories even in her First Nightmare.[34] She sealed the manor before leaving for the First Nightmare. She has a bit of money left. Most of their retainers fled over the years, and the rest left after her grandmother passed away.[75]
